# Past-Year Study Pack (learner surface)

## Register

product

## Product purpose

A single-purpose study surface for students working through past-year exam papers. The student reads a question, commits to an answer mentally, then reveals. The interface should disappear into recall and checking, not into pipeline metadata or reviewer tooling.

## Users

- University students revising before finals
- Often studying alone, late evening, on a laptop at a desk or in bed
- Already have the PDF papers; this artifact is the answer companion tied to lecture material
- Wants fast jumps between questions, clear reveal feedback, zero clutter

## Strategic principles

1. **Study first, audit never.** HTML is for learners. Verifier reports, page refs, and build metadata stay in JSON only.
2. **One paper at a time.** Each past-year paper is its own view, not a section in an endless scroll.
3. **Reveal teaches.** Objective questions mark the correct option in place; explanation follows. Structured questions expand a model answer. No duplicate "Answer: B." lines.
4. **Paper, not dashboard.** The surface should feel like working through an exam script, not a SaaS analytics product.
5. **Motion carries state.** Animations confirm reveal and navigation. They never decorate.

## Tone

Calm, direct, exam-room honest. Short labels. No hype, no "powered by" language, no pipeline voice.

## Anti-references

- Sage-green card-stack study apps (mint floating cards)
- Verifier dashboards (stat grids, "answered from source" badges, source-basis footers in HTML)
- Long single-page dumps with anchor-scroll paper tabs
- Dark terminal / neon "hacker" study themes
- Navy-and-gold academic prestige palettes
- Icon-card feature grids, hero metrics, modals for answers

## Success criteria

- Student can pick a paper, jump to any question, reveal, and read only what helps them learn
- Correct MCQ feedback is obvious without reading a separate answer line
- Revealed jump chips show progress at a glance
- Design feels intentional and distinct from generic AI study UIs
